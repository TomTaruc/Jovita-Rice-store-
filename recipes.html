Lugaw is a classic Filipino rice porridge that’s warm, comforting, and perfect for any time of the day. Made with glutinous rice simmered slowly in a flavorful broth of garlic, ginger, and onions, it has a smooth, hearty texture and a delicate balance of savory flavors. Often garnished with boiled eggs, fried garlic bits, and a squeeze of calamansi, Lugaw offers a simple yet satisfying experience. It’s a staple comfort food in the Philippines, enjoyed during rainy days or as a nourishing meal when feeling under the weather.

  • 1 cup glutinous rice, rinsed
  • 4 cups chicken broth or water
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of ginger, sliced into thin strips
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce or salt, to taste
  • Ground black pepper, to taste
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs, peeled and halved
  • Fried garlic bits, for garnish
  • Chopped green onions, for garnish
  • Calamansi or lemon wedges, for serving
  1. Sauté aromatics: Cook garlic until golden, set some aside for garnish. Add onion and ginger, cook until soft.
  2. Cook rice: Stir in rinsed glutinous rice and cook for 1-2 minutes.
  3. Simmer: Add chicken broth or water and simmer, stirring occasionally, until the rice softens and the mixture thickens (25-30 mins).
  4. Season: Add fish sauce or salt and ground black pepper to taste.
  5. Serve: Top with boiled eggs, fried garlic, green onions, and a squeeze of calamansi. Enjoy!

Champorado is a beloved Filipino chocolate rice porridge, made by simmering glutinous rice with cocoa powder until thick and creamy. This sweet, comforting dish is often enjoyed with a drizzle of evaporated or condensed milk and a touch of sugar for added richness. Traditionally served warm, champorado is perfect for breakfast or an afternoon snack and sometimes paired with dried fish (tuyo) for a unique contrast of flavors.

  • 1 cup glutinous rice, rinsed
  • 4 cups water
  • 1/2 cup cocoa powder (unsweetened)
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 cup condensed milk or evaporated milk, for topping
  • Optional: dried fish (tuyo) for a savory pairing
  1. Cook the Rice: In a pot, bring water to a boil and add the rinsed glutinous rice. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
  2. Add Chocolate: Once the rice starts to soften, dissolve the cocoa powder in a bit of warm water and pour it into the pot. Stir well until fully combined.
  3. Sweeten: Add sugar and continue simmering until the rice is fully cooked and the mixture thickens. Adjust sweetness to your preference.
  4. Serve: Ladle the champorado into bowls and drizzle with condensed or evaporated milk on top. For a traditional touch, serve with crispy dried fish (tuyo) on the side.

Egg fried rice is a simple yet flavorful dish that combines day-old rice with scrambled eggs, garlic, and savory seasonings. The rice is stir-fried to perfection, creating a light, fluffy texture with bits of tender egg evenly mixed in. A dash of soy sauce and green onions add depth and a hint of freshness to the dish. Quick and easy to prepare, egg fried rice is a comforting staple that works both as a satisfying main dish or a versatile side.

  • 2 cups cooked, day-old rice
  • 2 large eggs, beaten
  • 2 tbsp cooking oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp salt, or to taste
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 2 tbsp green onions, chopped (optional)
  1. Prepare the eggs: Heat 1 tbsp oil in a pan over medium heat. Scramble the beaten eggs until just set, then remove and set aside.
  2. Sauté the garlic: In the same pan, add the remaining oil and cook the garlic until golden and fragrant.
  3. Fry the rice: Add the day-old rice, breaking up any clumps. Stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until heated through.
  4. Season: Pour in the soy sauce, salt, and pepper, mixing well to evenly coat the rice.
  5. Combine: Return the scrambled eggs to the pan and stir everything together. Cook for another minute.
  6. Garnish and serve: Sprinkle with green onions, if using, and serve hot.

Tapsilog is a beloved Filipino breakfast dish that brings together savory, sweet, and garlicky flavors in one hearty plate. It consists of tapa — thinly sliced, marinated beef that’s fried to tender perfection — paired with sinangag (garlic fried rice) and a sunny-side-up itlog (fried egg). Often served with a side of vinegar dipping sauce or atchara (pickled papaya), tapsilog offers a satisfying balance of textures and tastes. It’s a classic comfort meal that’s popular any time of day.

  • For the Beef Tapa:
    • 500g beef sirloin, thinly sliced
    • 3 tbsp soy sauce
    • 2 tbsp calamansi or lemon juice
    • 1 tbsp sugar
    • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • For the Garlic Rice:
    • 4 cups cooked rice
    • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • For the Egg:
    • 4 eggs
  • Optional:
    • Vinegar dipping sauce
    • Atchara (pickled papaya)
  1. Marinate the Beef: Combine beef, soy sauce, calamansi juice, sugar, and garlic. Marinate for at least 1 hour.
  2. Cook the Tapa: Heat oil in a pan and cook the marinated beef until browned and tender. Set aside.
  3. Make Garlic Rice: In the same pan, sauté garlic until golden brown. Add cooked rice and stir well until evenly coated and heated through.
  4. Fry the Eggs: Cook eggs sunny-side-up or to your preferred doneness.
  5. Assemble and Serve: Plate the garlic rice, tapa, and fried egg together. Add vinegar dipping sauce or atchara on the side if desired. Enjoy!
